Our Mission
Birds in Flight Sanctuary was founded in 1991 to provide rescue, rehabilitation, and medical care of sick, injured, and orphaned birds of prey and waterfowl. Building the Birds in Flight Sanctuary of today that offers all wildlife a place to rest their paws and claws. Through public education, research, and caring treatment, we hope to ensure their future for years to come.
Helping A Dream Take Flight
One of the most unusual wildlife sanctuaries in northeast Ohio is poised to grow, thanks to a gift from the Cafaro Foundation. Birds in Flight Sanctuary has received a gift of $100,000 to help it establish a large, efficient facility to provide care for hundreds of animals and to create an education center for local school children and adults. Birds in Flight currently cares for more than 1,000 birds and other wild animals in a crowded rental home on a one-acre lot in a residential section of Howland Township. While a larger location in Howland would be a preferred option, the organization is considering other potential sites in Trumbull County.

We do not receive any state, federal or county money. All Funding is from Private donors.
Birds in Flight Sanctuary is a Non-Profit Organization as defined in Section 501(c)(3) of the IRS code.
